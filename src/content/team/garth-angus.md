**Garth Angus, founder and tour guide:**

I am a born and bred South African. My family moved to Cape Town 25 years ago, so although not a Capetonian by birth, this city is my home. What a privilege it is to now call places like Table Mountain, Company's Garden, Cape of Good Hope, and the Cape Winelands my "office". The Cape Peninsula is one of the world's most exquisitely beautiful places, and it's my stomping ground!

As a tourist guide in Cape Town, my working days are spent out and about with interested people who are relaxed and on vacation, and I'm showing them around some of these special places. One of the things I have come to learn over the years is that interested people are themselves interesting, and that doubles the reward of my career: I'm sharing the beauty of my city and surrounds, whilst at the same time learning from my guests' life experiences and opinions, their cities, countries, politics, and socio-economic challenges; we are all inter-connected.

My special interest as a tour guide is history and looking at it within a socio-economic and political context. I have read extensively on South African history – from the early days of the voyages of discovery until the present – and I am fascinated by the different periods of Cape (and South African) history. Where possible, I contextualise our stories on the southern tip of Africa with similar events that have happened around the world, often at similar times. This includes topics such as colonialization, the rise and fall of the Dutch East India Company, slavery, the struggle for human rights (our new democracy, Mandela), and civil conflicts.

I also love nature and the outdoors and live an active life: hiking (Table Mountain or in the Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve), running, cycling, tennis, and lately cold water swimming in one of the many spectacular tidal pools we have around the peninsula. When my guests have similar active interests, I love being able to incorporate a walk, a hike, a swim, or even a game of tennis! I also love appreciating the many fine wines that we produce in the Cape and introducing them to my guests.
