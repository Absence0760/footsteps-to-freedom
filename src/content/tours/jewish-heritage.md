Jews have been an extremely influential part in Cape Town and have made important contributions to the commercial, cultural and civic life of the city. Through art galleries and libraries, schools, through active participation in politics (the city has had six Jewish mayors), as well as through their very vocal opposition to Apartheid, the Cape Town Jewish community has made a lasting impact.

The Cape Town Hebrew Congregation was the first in South Africa and dates back to 1841. A few years later, the first synagogue (which still stands today), was built in 1849.

Our half-day tour includes a city orientation drive followed by a short walking tour ending with a visit to the Jewish campus. We start by driving to the District Six site which was the first home of many of the Lithuanian Jewish refugees escaping the pogroms of Eastern Europe in the early twentieth century.

We then head to the city centre where the tour resumes on foot. Whilst meandering your way through the city past our many historical buildings, your guide will tell the story.

Points of interest along the walking tour include:

- Parliament (Helen Suzman)
- St. George's Cathedral (first Jewish wedding)
- Rhodes (Diamonds and Barney Barnato)
- Walk ends with a visit to the Jewish campus where one of the guides from the museum will take guests through: The Holocaust Centre; S.A Jewish Museum; the Great (old) Synagogue and the new synagogue (except on Fridays)
- Optional extra: refreshments or lunch at Café Riteve (on the Jewish campus)
