The Footsteps to Freedom historic city walking tour is a unique opportunity to explore the vibrant and historic city of Cape Town, South Africa. This three-hour walking tour takes you through the bustling streets of downtown Cape Town, allowing you to experience the sights, sounds, and smells of this historic city first-hand. Your knowledgeable and experienced guide will provide an in-depth historical overview of the city, highlighting four key periods of its history: from the voyages of discovery until Dutch (VOC) settlement, VOC occupation of the Cape, British colonialism, and independent South Africa.

As you walk, you will learn about the key figures who have shaped the city's history, such as Mandela, Tutu, De Klerk, Smuts, and Cecil Rhodes. These stories, along with the history of slavery and apartheid, will provide you with a vivid and detailed understanding of the often-dramatic past of South Africa. Additionally, your guide will highlight parallel events that were taking place in other parts of the world, often at the same time that they were occurring in Cape Town and South Africa, to give you a more global perspective on the city's history.

Depending on guests' specific interests, we can adapt our walking tour to offer a number of niche walking tours:

- Mandela in Cape town: from prisoner to president
- Bo-Kaap and slavery tour, which looks at the Malay and Muslim communities and their influence on the city. Optional extra is to add on a cookery or samoosa-folding course.
- District Six museum which offers tours by ex-residents from the area who share their personal stories of living in and being forcible removed from District Six (additional cost for the museum visit).
- Jewish-interest tour (additional cost to visit the Jewish Museum)
